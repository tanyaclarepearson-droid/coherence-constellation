Developmental Frameworks

Page 2 • Mechanics, Movement, and Permanence
The constellation has three core developmental lenses. They work together: 3F describes how conditions are shaped, CREA describes how growth moves, and DPZ describes when growth begins to hold.
Mechanics Lens

3F

Form • Frequency • Fidelity

3F names what is offered, how often it recurs, and how coherently it is experienced over time.

  • Form: the shape of support
  • Frequency: repetition, timing, emotional valence, and practice cycles
  • Fidelity: coherence of experience, not rigid compliance
Movement Lens

CREA

Conditions → Resonance → Emergence → Agency

CREA describes how supportive conditions resonate, new capacities emerge, and agency becomes more possible.

  • Conditions become felt
  • Resonance creates meaning
  • Emergence becomes visible
  • Agency organizes action
Durability Lens

DPZ

Developmental Permanence Zone

DPZ asks when growth begins to hold without constant external pressure.

  • What is beginning to stay?
  • What still collapses under strain?
  • What conditions need protection?
  • What lasts because it is held?

How the lenses work together

3F names the patterning of conditions. CREA names the developmental movement those conditions make possible. DPZ names the threshold where growth becomes more stable, durable, and self-sustaining.

3FPattern the conditions
CREANotice the movement
DPZProtect what holds
PracticeReturn and tune again
“Growth is not only what appears. It is what begins to hold when the conditions are coherent enough to sustain it.”
